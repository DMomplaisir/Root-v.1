KNOW YOUR Rights

When PROTESTING you have the right to:
  1. Peacefully assemble to exercise your First Amendment right to protest.
  2. Protest in traditional "public forums" such as streets, sidewalks, and parks so lons as you aren't blocking traffic.
  3. Protest without a permit in response to recent events.
  4. Distribute leaflets and other literature on public sidewalks without a permit.
  5. Photograph or videotape cops.
>>Learn more: aclu.org/kyr-protest<<

When STOPPED BY POLICE you have the right to:
  1. Don't run or argue, even if the police are violating your rights
  2. Ask if you're free to leave. If you're under arrest, you have a right to know why.
  3. You have the right to remain silent.
  4. You can refuse consent for a search, but officers may "pat down" your clothing if they suspect a weapon.
>>Learn more: aclu.org/kyr-police<<
